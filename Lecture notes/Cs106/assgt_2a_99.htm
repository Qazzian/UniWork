CS10610 : 1999-00 Assignment 2 Part A

The second assignment will contribute 25% to the assessment for CS10610. Part A is the design phase. Part B is the implementation. The two parts carry equal weighting.

Scenario: A dance school specialising in Scottish Country Dancing has given you a contract to create an Access 2000 database management system. You have been supplied with four examples of leaflets that the club sends out to advertise balls and an example of information about bands. These examples contain the information that should be stored within the database.

PART A: Design Issues (12.5% overall)

I would recommend the following approach:

  1. Examine the data carefully

  2. List the main entity (entities)

  3. If more than one entity, then investigate the relationships between them

You should submit your design for the database as follows (each no more than one page in length including any diagrams):

    • a statement of originality (see Student Handbook)

    • your entity-relationship diagram, which should be neatly hand drawn

    • a list of proposed tables, specifying the fields and including primary and foreign keys, in the format shown below
      TABLE_A{field1, field2, field3*, …etc}
      The primary key field(s) is(are) underlined
      * foreign key linking to field.. in TABLE_B

    • for each table, a list of the proposed fields, with their proposed data type and size and any other field properties that you consider to be important with respect to the given data

    • a discussion about the fields you might have considered as a candidate for the primary key of each table. Defend your final choice of key.

This part of the second assignment may be neatly hand written.
