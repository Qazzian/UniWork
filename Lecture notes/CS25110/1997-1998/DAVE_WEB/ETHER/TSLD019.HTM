Probabilistic Characteristics of CSMA/CD

All access to the 802.3 LAN only completes with some probability. It is thus impossible to guarantee transfer rates. When the net is very busy collisions might go on forever !

Real Time Use

Debates often take place on the usability of CSMA/CD LANs for real time use. The answer depends on the true use intended rather than the LAN.

if load is strictly controlled then we can get specific delay and transfer times to a high degree of probability - but not guaranteed.

the robot example:

if control strategy is keep moving till you are told to stop then a disaster might occur.

if however the robot only keeps moving if it keeps getting messaged then we are fail safe in the “unlikely” event of a delay.

Slotted systems such as Cambridge ring can guarantee delivery delay and rates but at the expense of wasted bandwidth if the traffic is not constant.
