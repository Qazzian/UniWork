Computer Science CS25010: COMPUTER COMMUNICATIONS

Time allowed: 2 hours

Answer THREE from FIVE questions

  1. It is widely recognised that the development and adoption of standards is a critical activity in the world of computer communications.

    1. What types of organisations are involved in the creation of standards and from where do they draw their members? [9 Marks]

    2. Explain why the standards process can be time consuming. [6 marks]

    3. Explain what is meant by de-facto standards and their importance to users. [6 marks]

    4. Explain the importance of the ISO Open Systems Connection (OSI) Model and describe the roles of the layers which it includes. [12 marks]

  2. This question is concerned with basic terminology and concepts of computer communications.

    1. Briefly explain the meaning of the following pairs of technical terms

      (i)   digital and analogue [3 marks]
      (ii)  serial and parallel [3 marks]
      (iii) Local Area Network and Wide Area Network [6 marks]

    2. Explain the role of a modem and briefly describe either amplitude or frequency modulation. [8 marks]

    3. Briefly describe the hybrid modulation technique known as amplitude modulated phase shift keying. [5 marks]

    4. Outline some of the reasons why there are real limits on the maximum rate at which a modem can transmit data via the public switched telephone network (PSTN). What formulas have Shannon, Hartley and Nyquist developed to express these limits? [8 marks]

  3. In recent years the networking of computers has become a critical issue in the operation of many organizations. Ethernet is one of the most widely available technologies used to provide communication within a small geographic region such as a department, building, or campus.

    1. With reference to the local area network (LAN) known as Ethernet, explain the role of each of the following networking components:[12 marks]

      (i)   terminator
      (ii)  repeater
      (iii) bridge
      (iv)  transceiver (MAU)

    2. Describe the access protocol known as CSMA/CD utilized for 10BASE2 Ethernet. Include in your answer the characteristics of a network using this protocol and the reasons why a minimum packet size is required for its correct operation. [12 marks]

    3. Outline the truncated binary exponential backoff algorithm used when two stations attempt to transmit almost simultaneously. Propose reasons why this ensures good network behaviour under conditions of heavy load. [9 marks]

  4. The Integrated Services Digital Network (ISDN) is now widely available from telecommunications operators in many countries of the world. Many other services are also available which are provided mainly for data use.

    1. Briefly describe ISDN and what facilities basic rate access provides to a user. [7 marks]

    2. Explain what is meant by each of the following ISDN services, including examples where appropriate: [12 marks]

      (i)   Bearer service
      (ii)  Teleservice service
      (iii) Supplementary service

    3. Describe what factors a network manager might consider when deciding whether ISDN or leased lines might provide the most appropriate communications links for use within a new project. [10 marks]

    4. When choosing between leased lines, when might analogue lines be preferred over digital lines and vice-versa? [4 marks]

  5. This question is concerned with the topic known as Information Theory.

    1. Explain informally why the data content and information content of a message are not necessarily the same. Explain what is meant by the entropy of a message source. [3 marks]

    2. Explain what is meant by a variable length code. Outline in your answer why a variable length code might provide a more efficient set of codewords for an alphabet of symbols than a fixed length code. [5 marks]

    3. An alphabet consists of 7 symbols A to G inclusive. The letter A occurs with probability 6/16, both B and C occur with probability 3/16, the remainder all have probability 1/16.

      1. What is the minimum codeword size possible using a fixed length binary coding? [2 marks]

      2. Produce a Huffman coding for these symbols. [10 marks]

      3. Calculate the average code length for a symbol encoded using the Huffman code developed in part (ii). [4 marks]

    4. Many compression algorithms used for video encoding succeed in compressing a data source far below the theoretical limit calculated from its entropy.

      1. Why is this possible? [4 marks]

      2. Which applications would this approach be suitable for and which applications would we not expect to compress below the theoretical limit given by the entropy? Give reasons for your answers. [5 marks]
