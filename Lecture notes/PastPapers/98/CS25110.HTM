Time allowed: 2 hours

Answer THREE from FIVE questions

  1. A small company with two branch offices has been in existence for 10 years and is a provider of educational services. Until now, it has focused mainly on the design and production of paper based materials and it also provides telephone support. The company has decided to modernise its offerings and intends to provide web based materials including audio and video footage and intends to offer videophone based support to its clients. The company intends to dispose of all its telephones, fax machines and typewriters and equip each of its workers with one multi-functional PC-based workstation. This equipment should also be used to enable staff at the two offices to co-operate on joint projects. The telecommunications equipment in the towns where the two offices are located have recently been modernised and so all common services are now available.

    1. Produce a brief description of an appropriate workstation, focusing mainly on the peripheral devices and supporting interfaces as well as the various communications interfaces that are likely to be needed. [12 Marks]

    2. Describe the various application and system software components that are going to be required in the workstation to support its use by the workers. [12 marks]

    3. What role should standards within the workstation play in this particular case study? Your answer should include reference to the role of proprietary specifications in providing a basis for connecting the company's machines to those of its clients as well as for ensuring future upgrades. [9 marks]

  2. In recent years the networking of computers has become a critical issue in the operation of many organizations. Ethernet is one of the most widely available technologies used to provide communication within a small geographic region such as a department, building, or campus.

    1. With reference to the local area network (LAN) known as Ethernet, explain the role of each of the following networking components: [12 marks]

      (i)   terminator
      (ii)  repeater
      (iii) bridge
      (iv) transceiver (MAU)

    2. Describe the access protocol known as CSMA/CD utilized for 10BASE2 Ethernet. Include in your answer the characteristics of a network using this protocol and the reasons why a minimum packet size is required for its correct operation. [12 marks]

    3. Outline the truncated binary exponential backoff algorithm used when two stations attempt to transmit almost simultaneously. Propose reasons why this ensures good network behaviour under conditions of heavy load. [9 marks]

  3. Optical fibre media have led to massive improvements in the speed and reliability of Wide Area Networks, leading in turn to several new technologies such as Asynchronous Transfer Mode (ATM) and Frame Relay.

    1. Outline the major differences between traditional packet switching, frame relay and ATM technologies. [8 marks]

    2. The concept of a Virtual Circuit is important for several high speed technologies. Explain what is meant by the term Virtual Circuit and how this differs from a pure datagram approach. [6 marks]

    3. Explain why Frame Relay is more efficient than traditional Packet switching when used over transmission media which have a low probability of errors. [10 marks]

    4. Briefly discuss how an ATM network can support a wider range of applications than networks constructed using packet switching or Frame relay. [9 marks]

  4. The Integrated Services Digital Network (ISDN) is now widely available from telecommunications operators in many countries of the world.

    1. Briefly describe ISDN and what facilities basic rate access provides to a user. [7 marks]

    2. Explain what is meant by each of the following ISDN services, including examples where appropriate: [12 marks]

      (i)   Bearer service
      (ii)  Teleservice service
      (iii) Supplementary service

    3. Explain the term flow control and the mechanisms which might be used to provide it on a full duplex channel. [7 marks]

    4. Explain the term error control and the mechanisms which might be used to provide it on a full duplex channel. [7 marks]

  5. This question is concerned with the topic known as Information Theory.

    1. Explain informally why the data content and information content of a message are not necessarily the same. Explain what is meant by the entropy of a message source. [3 marks]

    2. Explain what is meant by a variable length code. Outline in your answer why a variable length code might provide a more efficient set of codewords for an alphabet of symbols than a fixed length code. [5 marks]

    3. An alphabet consists of 7 symbols A to G inclusive. The letter A occurs with probability 6/16, both B and C occur with probability 3/16, the remainder all have probability 1/16.

      1. What is the minimum codeword size possible using a fixed length binary coding? [2 marks]

      2. Produce a Huffman coding for these symbols. [10 marks]

      3. Calculate the average code length for a symbol encoded using the Huffman code developed in part (ii). [4 marks]

    4. Many compression algorithms used for video encoding succeed in compressing a data source far below the theoretical limit calculated from its entropy.

      1. Why is this possible? [4 marks]

      2. Which applications would this approach be suitable for and which applications would we not expect to compress below the theoretical limit given by the entropy? Give reasons for your answers. [5 marks]
